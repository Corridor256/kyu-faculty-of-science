Research at the Faculty of Science

The Faculty of Science at Kyambogo University is committed to conducting cutting-edge research that addresses local, regional, and global challenges. Our research spans multiple scientific disciplines including chemistry, physics, biology, mathematics, and science education.

Faculty members and students collaborate on projects that range from environmental sustainability and public health to renewable energy and educational pedagogy. We actively seek partnerships with national and international research institutions.

Research Focus Areas

Environmental Chemistry

Investigating water quality, soil contamination, pesticide residues, and sustainable waste management solutions for Uganda and East Africa.

Water Quality Soil Analysis Green Chemistry

Renewable Energy

Research on solar energy systems, biogas technology, and energy-efficient materials to support Uganda's clean energy transition.

Solar Cells Biogas Energy Storage

Genetics & Molecular Biology

Studies in genetic diversity, crop improvement, disease resistance, and molecular diagnostics for tropical diseases.

Genomics Crop Science Diagnostics

Biodiversity & Conservation

Documenting and preserving Uganda's rich biodiversity through ecological surveys, habitat assessment, and community-based conservation programs.

Ecology Wildlife Wetlands

Science Education Research

Investigating effective teaching methods, curriculum design, technology integration, and student learning outcomes in science classrooms.

Pedagogy STEM Assessment

Applied Mathematics & Statistics

Mathematical modeling of infectious diseases, population dynamics, climate patterns, and statistical analysis for social research.

Modeling Data Science Epidemiology

Our Academic Programs

The Faculty of Science offers comprehensive programs designed to produce competent science educators and researchers. Select a level below to explore.

BSc. Ed

ESP — Chemistry & Mathematics

3 Years | Full Time

Program Overview

This program trains students to become competent secondary school teachers in Chemistry and Mathematics. It combines deep content knowledge in both subjects with pedagogical skills and school practice experience.

Chemistry Courses
  • General Chemistry I & II
  • Organic Chemistry I & II
  • Physical Chemistry
  • Analytical Chemistry
  • Kinetics & Electrochemistry
  • Chemistry Methods & Research
Mathematics Courses
  • Calculus I, II & III
  • Linear Algebra
  • Numerical Analysis
  • Probability Theory
  • Abstract Algebra
  • Mathematics Education
Education Courses
  • Educational Psychology
  • General Methods
  • Educational Technology
  • Learning Measurement
  • School Practice I & II
  • Curriculum Studies
Entry Requirements
UACE with at least 2 principal passes Chemistry at principal level Mathematics at principal or subsidiary level Minimum of 2 subsidiary passes

MSc.

Master of Science in Chemistry

2 Years | Full/Part Time

Program Overview

An advanced program focusing on specialized areas of chemistry including organic synthesis, environmental chemistry, and materials science. Students conduct original research and submit a thesis.

Entry Requirements
Bachelor's degree in Chemistry or related field Minimum of Second Class Upper division Two academic referees
MSc.

Master of Science in Physics

PGDE

Postgraduate Diploma in Education

1 Year | Full Time

Program Overview

Designed for science graduates who wish to obtain teaching qualifications. Covers pedagogy, curriculum design, assessment, and supervised school practice.

Entry Requirements
Bachelor's degree in a science discipline Minimum of Second Class Lower division
PhD

Doctor of Philosophy in Science Education

3–5 Years | Full/Part Time

Program Overview

This doctoral program focuses on advancing knowledge in science education through original research. Areas include curriculum development, pedagogical innovations, assessment strategies, and STEM education policy.

Entry Requirements
Master's degree in a relevant discipline Published research is an advantage Detailed research proposal Two academic referees
PhD

Doctor of Philosophy in Chemistry / Physics / Biology / Mathematics

3–5 Years | Full/Part Time

Program Overview

Discipline-specific doctoral programs allowing in-depth research in specialized areas. Candidates work under experienced supervisors and contribute new knowledge to their respective fields through a doctoral thesis.

Entry Requirements
Master's degree in the relevant science discipline Strong research background Comprehensive research proposal

Academic Calendar Highlights

Semester I — August

Registration opens. Orientation week for new students. Lectures begin mid-August.

Mid-Semester Tests — October

Coursework tests, assignments, and practical assessments.

End of Semester Exams — December

Final examinations for Semester I. Results released in January.

Semester II — February

Registration and lectures resume. New course units begin.

School Practice — April/May

Year 2 and Year 3 students go for supervised teaching practice in secondary schools.

Graduation — July

Annual graduation ceremony at Kyambogo University main grounds.
